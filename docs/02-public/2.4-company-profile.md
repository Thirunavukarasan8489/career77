1. Purpose

The Career77 Company Profile page provides public information about a company and its available job opportunities.

Its primary objectives are:

Help Candidates learn about companies.
Help Public Visitors understand company information.
Display publicly available company details.
Display the company's active job openings.
Allow users to navigate from a company to its jobs.
Provide a trusted company discovery experience.
Support Candidate job discovery and conversion.

The primary journey is:

Company Discovery
    ↓
Company Profile
    ↓
Learn About Company
    ↓
View Open Jobs
    ↓
Select Job
    ↓
Job Detail
    ↓
Apply
2. Primary Users

The Company Profile page is publicly accessible.

Public Visitor
    ↓
View Company
Candidate
    ↓
View Company
    ↓
Explore Jobs
    ↓
Apply

Recruiters can manage their company information through the private Recruiter Portal.

The public Company Profile and private Company Management experience must remain separate.

5. Company Header

The Company Header is the primary information section.

It should display:

Company Logo
Company Name
Industry
Location
Verification Status

Example:

│                                     │
│        [ Company Logo ]             │

│        Career77 Technologies        │
│        Technology                   │
│        Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu       │
│        ✓ Verified Company           │
│                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

The most important company information must be immediately visible.

6. Company Logo

The Company Profile may display the company's public logo.

Requirements:

Use the approved company logo.
Optimize image loading.
Provide appropriate alternative text.
Handle missing logos gracefully.
Do not expose private storage paths.

If no logo is available, the interface should display an approved default company placeholder.

7. Company Name

The Company Name must clearly identify the organization.

The name must come from the approved company record.

The public page must not expose:

Internal company IDs.
Internal verification notes.
Private administrative metadata.

The public Company Name should be consistent across:

Company Profile
Job Detail
Job Search
Recruiter Portal
8. Industry

The Company Profile may display the company's industry.

Examples:

Technology
Education
Finance
Healthcare
Manufacturing
Retail

The industry value should come from the approved company data model.

The same taxonomy should be used consistently across the platform.

9. Company Location

The Company Profile may display the company's public location.

Example:

Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu

The displayed location must use publicly approved company information.

Private addresses must not be exposed.

10. Company Verification Status

Verified companies may display an appropriate verification indicator.

Example:

✓ Verified Company

The verification status must be controlled by the Super Admin verification workflow.

The system must not allow Recruiters to manually claim verification.

The verification indicator must only appear when the company has an approved verification status.

11. Company Overview

The Company Overview provides information about the organization.

Example:

About the Company

Career77 Technologies is a technology organization focused
on building modern digital solutions and software products.

The content should be based on the company's approved public profile.

The content must not expose confidential company information.

12. Company Information

The Company Profile may provide additional public company information.

Potential fields include:

Company Name
Industry
Location
Company Size
Website
Description

Only approved public fields should be displayed.

Empty or unavailable fields should not create unnecessary blank sections.

13. Company Website

If the company provides an approved public website, it may be displayed.

The external website link must:

Open safely.
Use an appropriate external-link treatment.
Not expose internal application URLs.
Be validated according to platform security rules.

Only approved company website information should be displayed.

14. Open Jobs

The Open Jobs section is a major conversion area.

The primary flow is:

Company Profile
    ↓
Open Jobs
    ↓
Job Card
    ↓
Job Detail
    ↓
Apply

Only publicly available active jobs should appear.

15. Open Jobs Summary

The page may display a summary such as:

12 Open Positions

The count must reflect the current number of public active jobs.

If there are no active jobs:

No open positions currently available.

The Company Profile should remain accessible even when there are no open jobs.

16. Company Job Card

Each open job may display:

Job Title
Location
Experience Level
Employment Type
Posted Date
View Job

Example:

┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Full Stack Developer                │
│ Coimbatore · Mid-Level · Full Time  │
│ Posted 2 days ago                   │
│                         View Job →  │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

The Job Card must link to the public Job Detail page.

17. Job Filtering on Company Profile

If the company has many active jobs, filtering may be provided.

Possible filters:

Experience
Location
Employment Type

The exact filters should be introduced only when they provide meaningful value.

The default Company Profile should remain simple and easy to navigate.

18. Job Pagination

If a company has many open positions, the Open Jobs section may use:

Pagination

or:

Load More

The implementation must be consistent with the Job Search experience.

The page must avoid loading an unnecessarily large number of jobs at once.

19. No Open Jobs State

When the company has no active jobs:

Company Profile
    ↓
Open Jobs
    ↓
No Open Positions

Example:

No open positions currently available.

Check back later for new opportunities.

The page may optionally provide:

Browse Other Jobs

This navigates to the general Job Search page.

20. Company Not Found

If the requested company does not exist:

Company URL
    ↓
Company Not Found

The system should display an appropriate error state.

Example:

Company not found.

The company may have been removed or is no longer publicly available.

Primary action:

[ Browse Jobs ]
21. Private Company State

If a company exists but is not publicly available:

Company Record
    ↓
Public Visibility Check
    ↓
Not Public

The system must not expose private company information.

The public request should return an appropriate Not Found or unavailable state.

Internal company status details must not be revealed.

22. Suspended Company State

If a company has been suspended:

Company
    ↓
Suspended
    ↓
Public Access Restricted

The platform must prevent normal public access to the company's profile where required.

The system must not expose internal suspension reasons.

23. Company Profile URL

Each publicly visible company should have a unique public URL.

Conceptual route:

/companies/[company-slug]

The URL should be:

Stable.
Shareable.
Human-readable where possible.
SEO-friendly.

The exact routing structure must follow the approved frontend architecture.

28. Company Profile Error State

If the Company Profile API fails:

Company Request
    ↓
API Error

Display:

Unable to load company information.

Please try again.

Primary action:

[ Retry ]

Internal system information must not be exposed.

29. Company Profile API

The frontend should retrieve public company information through an approved API.

Conceptual endpoint:

GET /api/companies/:id

or:

GET /api/companies/:slug

The exact endpoint must follow the Career77 API specification.

The API must:

Validate the company identifier.
Check public visibility.
Check company status.
Return approved public fields.
Return public active jobs where applicable.
Handle missing companies.
Handle database errors.

31. Public Company Data Security

The Company Profile API must never expose:

Recruiter passwords.
Authentication tokens.
Private recruiter information.
Internal verification notes.
Private company documents.
Billing information.
Subscription information.
Internal administrative data.
Private employee information.

Only approved public company data may be returned.

32. Recruiter Company Management

Recruiters manage their company information through the private Recruiter Portal.

The public Company Profile is a read-only public representation.

Recruiter management flow:

Recruiter
    ↓
Recruiter Portal
    ↓
Company
    ↓
Edit Company
    ↓
Save
    ↓
Public Profile Updated

Public changes must follow the appropriate verification and moderation rules.

Not every Recruiter update should automatically become publicly visible if approval is required.

41. Company Profile Acceptance Criteria

The Company Profile feature is complete when:

Public users can access publicly visible companies.
Candidates can access publicly visible companies.
Company logo is displayed where available.
Company name is displayed.
Industry is displayed where available.
Location is displayed where available.
Public company description is displayed.
Verification status is displayed when approved.
Public website is displayed where available.
Active public jobs are displayed.
Job count is accurate.
Users can navigate to Job Detail.
Users can navigate from Job Detail back to Company Profile.
Users can browse other jobs.
Empty job states are handled.
Loading states are handled.
Error states are handled.
Not Found states are handled.
Private companies are not exposed.
Suspended companies are appropriately restricted.
Private recruiter information is never exposed.
Company information is responsive.
Accessibility requirements are met.
SEO requirements are implemented.
Public API responses are secure.
Recruiter management is separated from public Company Profile.
Company verification is controlled by the Super Admin workflow.
